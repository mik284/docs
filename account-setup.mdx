Before your team processes a single order, take a few minutes to complete your shop setup. This section walks you through every tab inside **System Setup** — from your business profile and bank details to printer configuration and receipt appearance. Getting these settings right upfront ensures that receipts print correctly, notifications reach the right people, and your brand is represented consistently across the POS interface.

<Warning>
  Each tab in System Setup has its own **Save** button. Changes you make on one tab are **not** automatically saved when you switch to another. Always click the save button on each tab before moving on, or your changes will be lost.
</Warning>

## Shop Profile

Your shop profile contains the core business information that appears on receipts, in reports, and on the customer-facing display.

Navigate to **Settings > System Setup** and select the **System Profile** tab. Fill in the following fields:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Business Name** | Your trading name as it appears on receipts and the dashboard. |
| **Email** | Business email used for notifications and customer communications. |
| **Phone** | Primary contact number printed on receipts and used for WhatsApp alerts. |
| **Location** | Physical address printed on receipts and invoices. |
| **Social Link** | Your business social media profile URL (optional). |
| **KRA Pin** | Your Kenya Revenue Authority PIN, printed on tax receipts (Kenya). |
| **P.O. Box** | Postal address, printed on formal receipts (optional). |

The tab also lets you link your M-Pesa payment method (Till number or Paybill details) so that the correct payment information appears on printed receipts.

Click **Submit** (or **Update** if you are editing existing details) when all fields are complete.

## Bank Details

Bank details appear on printed invoices and formal receipts, making it easier for corporate customers to process payments by bank transfer. You can add multiple bank accounts and mark one as the primary account.

Navigate to **Settings > System Setup** and select the **Bank Details** tab. Click **Add Bank** to open the bank entry form and fill in the following:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Bank Name** | The full name of your business bank (e.g. *Equity Bank Kenya*). |
| **Account Number** | Your business current account number. |
| **Account Name** | The name on the bank account as it appears on statements. |
| **Branch** | The branch name or code where the account is held. |
| **SWIFT Code** | Required for international wire transfers (optional for local use). |
| **Paybill No.** | Your M-Pesa Paybill number if payments are collected via Paybill (optional). |

Click **Save Changes** after adding or editing your bank details. If you have more than one bank account, use the **Set as Primary** link on any card to choose which account prints on receipts by default.

## Printer Settings

BasePoint POS routes print jobs through a lightweight **Print Agent** application that you install on the computer connected to your thermal printer. This approach means the browser never needs direct access to a local printer driver.

Navigate to **Settings > System Setup** and select the **Printer Settings** tab. The tab has three sub-sections:

**Connected Agents** — shows every Print Agent currently online for your shop. Once an agent is running, it appears here automatically. Use the **Assign Category** button on each agent card to map a product main-category (e.g. *Kitchen*, *Bar*, *Cashier*) to that printer. When an order is placed, items are routed to the agent assigned to their main category.

**Download Agent** — provides one-click downloads of the Print Agent installer for macOS (Apple Silicon) and Windows (64-bit). After downloading, run the installer, launch the app, enter your **Company Code** and **Shop ID**, then click **Connect**. The agent will appear in the Connected Agents list within seconds.

**Printing Controls** — two global toggles that control printing behaviour across the entire POS:

- **Captain Order Mode** — when enabled, a **Send** button appears in the cart drawer so staff can send orders to the kitchen printer without completing the full checkout flow.
- **Print Method** — switch between *Browser* printing (the browser's built-in print dialog) and *API* printing (routed through connected Print Agents). Agent printing is only available when at least one agent is online.

<Note>
  The Print Agent app needs to be running on a machine that is on the same network as your thermal printer. You only need one agent per printer, but you can run multiple agents if you have printers in different stations (e.g. a kitchen printer and a cashier printer).
</Note>

## Receipt Appearance

Customise the font size and weight of your printed thermal receipts to match your printer and paper width.

Navigate to **Settings > System Setup** and select the **Receipt Appearance** tab. You can configure:

- **Font Size** — adjust using the slider or the preset dropdown (Small 10 px, Normal 13 px, Large 15 px, X-Large 17 px). Values above 15 px are clamped at print time to prevent text overflow on 80 mm rolls.
- **Text Weight** — choose between **Bold** (the default, recommended for thermal printers) and **Normal**.

A live preview in the tab shows exactly how a sample receipt will look with your chosen settings. Changes are saved automatically 600 milliseconds after you stop adjusting — no Save button required. Staff can still override these settings per print in the print modal, but those overrides are not persisted.

## Notification Settings

BasePoint POS can send notifications to customers and staff via email and WhatsApp when key events occur.

Navigate to **Settings > System Setup** and select the **Notification Settings** tab. Toggle each channel on or off:

- **Email** — send notifications via email (powered by Brevo). Make sure a valid email address is saved in the **System Profile** tab.
- **WhatsApp** — send notifications via WhatsApp (powered by Twilio). Requires a registered WhatsApp sender — see the **WhatsApp Registration** tab to set one up. You can also choose between **Sandbox Mode** (for testing, using the Twilio sandbox number) and **Production Mode** (for live customer messaging with your own registered number).

Click **Save Changes** to apply your notification channel preferences.

<Tip>
  If you enable WhatsApp notifications but have not yet registered a sender, the app will display a reminder in the Notification Settings tab directing you to the **WhatsApp Registration** tab. Complete that step first before switching WhatsApp to Production Mode.
</Tip>
