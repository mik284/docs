The Campaigns feature in Mteja CRM lets you send targeted messages to your customer base and measure the results. Whether you're promoting a seasonal offer, announcing a new menu, or re-engaging dormant customers, campaigns let you reach the right audience through the right channel — and track every response, lead, and sale that follows.

<Note>
  WhatsApp campaigns require your WhatsApp sender number to be registered in **System Setup > WhatsApp Registration** before you can launch. See [Notifications](/settings/notifications) for setup steps.
</Note>

## Creating a Campaign

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Campaigns page">
    Navigate to **CRM** in the left sidebar, then select **Campaigns**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new campaign">
    Click **New Campaign** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your campaign">
    Fill in the campaign details:

    - **Campaign Name** — a descriptive internal name (e.g. "March Happy Hour Promo")
    - **Type** — the delivery channel: **WhatsApp**, **Email**, or **SMS**
    - **Target Audience** — choose who receives the message:
      - *All Customers* — your entire customer database
      - *Filtered Segment* — apply filters such as last visit date, total spend, location, or customer tag
    - **Message Content** — the body of your message. Use template variables (see tip below) to personalize each message.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the schedule and budget">
    - **Start Date** — when the campaign launches
    - **End Date** — when the campaign closes (optional for one-time sends)
    - **Budget** — the planned spend for this campaign, used to calculate ROI in reporting
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the campaign">
    Review all settings, then click **Launch**. BasePoint POS queues the messages for delivery. You can monitor progress from the Campaigns dashboard immediately after launch.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Personalize every message using template variables such as `{{customer_name}}`, `{{shop_name}}`, and `{{promo_code}}`. A message that begins with "Hi Sarah, …" consistently outperforms a generic broadcast. Enter variables in double curly braces exactly as shown in the template editor.
</Tip>

## Campaign Types

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="WhatsApp Bulk Message" icon="message-circle">
    Send a rich-text or media message directly to customers' WhatsApp numbers. Requires a registered WhatsApp Business sender. Ideal for high-open-rate promotions and order follow-ups.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Email Newsletter" icon="mail">
    Deliver a formatted email to your customers' inboxes. Best for detailed announcements, menus, event invitations, and newsletters where longer content works well.
  </Card>
  <Card title="SMS Blast" icon="smartphone">
    Send a short plain-text message to customers' mobile numbers. High deliverability even without a smartphone. Great for flash sales, reminders, and time-sensitive alerts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Tracking Results

After a campaign launches, the Campaigns dashboard updates in real time with performance metrics. Click any campaign row to view its full report:

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| **Messages Sent** | Total number of messages dispatched |
| **Delivered** | Messages confirmed as delivered to recipients |
| **Opened** | Messages opened or read (WhatsApp read receipts / email open tracking) |
| **Responded** | Recipients who replied or clicked a link |
| **Leads Generated** | New CRM leads created as a result of campaign responses |
| **Revenue Attributed** | Sales linked to customers who received this campaign |

Use the **Revenue Attributed** metric to calculate your campaign ROI against the budget you set at creation.

<Accordion title="Why do delivered and opened counts differ?">
  Delivery confirms the message reached the recipient's device or inbox. Opens are tracked separately — for WhatsApp this uses read receipts (blue ticks), and for email it uses a tracking pixel. Recipients who have read receipts disabled or email image loading blocked will show as delivered but not opened. This is normal and expected.
</Accordion>
