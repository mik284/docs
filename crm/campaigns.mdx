The Campaigns feature in Mteja CRM lets you plan, execute, and measure targeted marketing activity across any channel — from email newsletters and SMS blasts to events, referral programmes, social media pushes, and more. Every campaign records its own budget, actual spend, leads generated, conversions, and revenue so you can see exactly which activities drive sales.

<Note>
  The Mteja CRM module must be enabled on your BasePoint POS subscription before Campaigns appear in your navigation. Contact your account manager or visit your plan settings to activate it.
</Note>

## Creating a Campaign

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Campaigns page">
    Navigate to **CRM** in the left sidebar, then select **Campaigns**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new campaign">
    Click **New Campaign** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your campaign">
    Fill in the campaign details:

    - **Campaign Name** — a descriptive internal name (e.g. "April SMS Blast")
    - **Type** — the marketing channel (see [Campaign Types](#campaign-types) below)
    - **Status** — the starting state; new campaigns default to **Draft**
    - **Start Date / End Date** — the campaign's active window
    - **Budget** — the planned spend in KES, used to calculate ROI
    - **Target Leads** — optional lead-count goal for the campaign
    - **Target Revenue** — optional revenue goal in KES
    - **Department** — optionally scope the campaign to a specific department
    - **Target Audience** — a free-text description of who you are targeting (e.g. "Existing customers aged 25–40")
    - **Tags** — comma-separated labels for filtering and reporting (e.g. "seasonal, promo")
    - **Description** — any additional notes about the campaign's goals or approach
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the campaign">
    Click **Create Campaign**. The campaign is saved in **Draft** status. Update the status to **Scheduled** or **Active** when you are ready to begin.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use **Tags** consistently across campaigns (e.g. "Q2", "upsell", "loyalty") so you can filter and compare related campaigns in the list view at a glance.
</Tip>

## Campaign Statuses

A campaign moves through a lifecycle as you work on it:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Being prepared; not yet active |
| **Scheduled** | Approved and queued to start on the set start date |
| **Active** | Currently running |
| **Paused** | Temporarily stopped; can be resumed |
| **Completed** | Finished and closed for reporting |
| **Cancelled** | Abandoned before completion |

## Tracking Results

Click any campaign row in the list to open its detail panel. The panel shows real-time performance metrics updated as you record actuals:

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| **Budget** | Planned spend set at campaign creation |
| **Spend** | Actual amount spent so far |
| **Leads** | Number of leads generated by this campaign |
| **Conversions** | Leads that converted to paying customers |
| **Revenue** | Actual revenue attributed to this campaign |
| **ROI** | `(Revenue − Spend) ÷ Spend × 100`, calculated automatically |

The detail panel also shows a **Lead Pipeline Breakdown** (how campaign leads are distributed across pipeline stages) and any **Sales Targets** or **Sales Budgets** linked to this campaign.

<Accordion title="How do I update actual spend and revenue?">
  Actual spend, leads, conversions, and revenue are updated manually or through linked sales targets and budgets. Open the campaign, click **Edit Campaign**, and update the actuals fields. You can also link a Sales Target or Sales Budget to this campaign from the Sales Targets and Sales Budgets pages — those records will then appear inside the campaign detail panel automatically.
</Accordion>
