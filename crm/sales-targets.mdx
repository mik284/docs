Sales Targets and Sales Budgets in Mteja CRM give you a structured way to set expectations, motivate your team, and measure whether your business is on track. Assign individual targets to staff members or set location-level targets for each shop, then watch actual performance update in real time against those goals.

<Note>
  Sales Targets and Budgets are part of the Mteja CRM module. Ensure the CRM module is enabled on your subscription to access these features.
</Note>

## Setting a Sales Target

Create a target for an individual staff member or for an entire shop location.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Sales Targets">
    Navigate to **CRM** in the left sidebar, then select **Sales Targets**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new target">
    Click **Add Target** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the target">
    Complete the target form:

    - **Target For** — select either a **Staff Member** (individual target) or a **Shop** (location target)
    - **Staff / Shop** — pick the specific person or location from the dropdown
    - **Period** — choose the time span:
      - *Monthly* — resets at the start of each calendar month
      - *Quarterly* — spans a three-month quarter
      - *Annual* — covers the full financial year
    - **Target Amount** — the revenue goal in your local currency
    - **Notes** — optional context, such as the rationale or linked incentive
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the target">
    Click **Save**. The target appears on the Sales Targets dashboard and begins tracking actual sales immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Review targets at the start of each quarter and adjust for seasonality. A café will naturally sell more in school holiday periods; a restaurant may peak around festive seasons. Setting realistic, season-adjusted targets keeps your team motivated rather than discouraged.
</Tip>

## Sales Budgets

Sales Budgets operate at a higher level than individual staff targets — they represent plan-level financial expectations by product category or product line for a given period.

To manage budgets, navigate to **CRM > Sales Budgets**. From here you can:

- Create a budget for a specific **product category** (e.g. Beverages, Food, Merchandise)
- Assign a **planned revenue amount** for the period
- Track **actual vs. budgeted** revenue as sales come in

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Staff Targets" icon="user">
    Assigned to individual team members. Used for performance management, commissions, and staff reviews.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sales Budgets" icon="chart-bar">
    Assigned to product categories or business lines. Used for financial planning, purchasing decisions, and board-level reporting.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Tracking Progress

The Sales Targets dashboard gives you an at-a-glance view of how your team is tracking:

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| **Actual Sales** | Total revenue for the period so far, pulled live from your POS transactions |
| **Target Amount** | The goal set for this person or location |
| **% Achieved** | Actual ÷ Target × 100, updated in real time |
| **Trend Chart** | Day-by-day or week-by-week revenue line showing trajectory toward the target |
| **Top Performers** | Ranked list of staff sorted by percentage of target achieved |

<Accordion title="How are sales attributed to a staff member?">
  Sales are attributed to the staff member who processed the transaction at the POS. This relies on each staff member logging in with their own credentials or PIN before processing a sale. If staff share a login, attribution will not be accurate — ensure every team member has their own account under **Settings > Staff & Users**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens when a target period ends?">
  At the end of a period, the target is archived with its final achieved amount. You can view historical performance from the Sales Targets page by filtering by past periods. Previous targets do not automatically roll over — create new targets for each fresh period.
</Accordion>
