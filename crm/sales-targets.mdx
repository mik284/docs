Sales Targets and Sales Budgets in Mteja CRM give you a structured way to set expectations, motivate your team, and measure whether your business is on track. Assign targets to individual staff members, roles, departments, or named teams, then watch actual performance update in real time against those goals.

<Note>
  Sales Targets and Budgets are part of the Mteja CRM module. Ensure the CRM module is enabled on your subscription to access these features. Contact your account manager or visit your plan settings to activate it.
</Note>

## Setting a Sales Target

Create a target for a staff member, a role, a department, or a named team.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Sales Targets">
    Navigate to **CRM** in the left sidebar, then select **Sales Targets**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new target">
    Click **New Target** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the target">
    Complete the target form:

    - **Target Name** — a descriptive label (e.g. "Q2 Revenue Target")
    - **Metric Type** — what you are measuring:
      - *Revenue* — total sales value
      - *Units Sold* — number of items sold
      - *Leads Generated* — new leads created in CRM
      - *Leads Converted* — leads that became customers
      - *Gross Profit* — revenue minus cost of goods sold
      - *New Customers* — first-time buyers in the period
    - **Period** — the time span: *Daily*, *Weekly*, *Monthly*, *Quarterly*, *Annual*, or *Custom*
    - **Period Start / Period End** — exact start and end dates for the target window
    - **Target Value** — the numeric goal (amount for revenue/profit targets, count for all others)
    - **Assign To** — who the target applies to:
      - *Specific User* — an individual staff member
      - *Role* — all users with a given role (e.g. all Sales Reps)
      - *Department* — all users in a department
      - *Team Label* — a free-text team name (e.g. "Field Sales", "Nairobi Team")
    - **Link to Campaign** *(optional)* — restrict the target to results from a specific campaign
    - **Notes** — optional context, such as the rationale or linked incentive
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the target">
    Click **Create Target**. The target appears on the Sales Targets list and begins tracking actual results immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Review targets at the start of each quarter and adjust for seasonality. A café will naturally sell more in school holiday periods; a restaurant may peak around festive seasons. Setting realistic, season-adjusted targets keeps your team motivated rather than discouraged.
</Tip>

## Reading the Targets List

The Sales Targets list gives you an at-a-glance view of how every target is tracking:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Target** | Target name, metric type, and period |
| **Scope** | The user, role, department, or team the target is assigned to |
| **Campaign** | The campaign this target is linked to, if any |
| **Target Value** | The goal set for the period |
| **Actual** | Current achieved value, pulled live from POS transactions and CRM data |
| **Achievement** | Percentage achieved, shown as a colour-coded progress bar (green ≥ 100%, amber ≥ 70%, red below 70%) |
| **Period** | The start and end dates of the target window |

Click the **⋮** menu on any row to **View Details** (full achievement breakdown) or **Edit Target**.

<Accordion title="How are sales attributed to a staff member?">
  Sales are attributed to the staff member who processed the transaction at the POS. This relies on each staff member logging in with their own credentials or PIN before processing a sale. If staff share a login, attribution will not be accurate — ensure every team member has their own account under **Settings > Staff & Users**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens when a target period ends?">
  At the end of a period, the target is archived with its final achieved amount. You can view historical performance from the Sales Targets page by searching or filtering. Previous targets do not automatically roll over — create new targets for each fresh period.
</Accordion>

## Sales Budgets

Sales Budgets operate alongside individual targets — they represent financial expectations at the department or campaign level and include a revenue, cost-of-goods, and expenses plan for a given period.

To manage budgets, navigate to **CRM > Sales Budgets**. From there you can:

- Create a budget and set **Budgeted Revenue**, **Budgeted COGS**, and **Budgeted Expenses**
- Optionally scope the budget to a **Department** or link it to a **Campaign**
- Track **Actual vs. Budgeted** figures for revenue, COGS, expenses, and gross profit as the period progresses
- Follow the approval workflow — budgets start as **Draft**, can be **Submitted** for manager review, then **Approved** or **Rejected**

### Budget Approval Workflow

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Budget is being prepared and can still be edited |
| **Submitted** | Sent for manager review; editing is locked |
| **Approved** | Confirmed by a manager; budget is live for tracking |
| **Rejected** | Returned for revision with a rejection reason |
| **Locked** | Finalised and closed; no further changes permitted |
