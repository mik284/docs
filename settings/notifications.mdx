Notifications keep you and your team informed of important business events without requiring anyone to be actively looking at the screen. Whether it's a low-stock warning that prevents a menu item from running out, a daily sales summary landing in your inbox each evening, or a WhatsApp receipt sent automatically to a customer — BasePoint POS notifications run quietly in the background so your business runs smoothly.

## Email Notifications

Configure email alerts in **Settings > System Setup > Notification Settings**.

The following notification types are available:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Daily Sales Summary" icon="chart-line">
    Sent at the end of each business day. Includes total revenue, number of transactions, average order value, and top-selling items.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Low Stock Alert" icon="triangle-alert">
    Triggered automatically when any product's quantity drops below its configured reorder level. Helps you reorder before you run out.
  </Card>
  <Card title="New Order Notification" icon="bell">
    Fires when a new order is placed — especially useful for online orders and delivery orders that arrive when the POS screen isn't being monitored.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Custom Alerts" icon="settings">
    Additional alert types may be available depending on your plan and enabled modules.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

**Setting up email recipients:**

1. Navigate to **Settings > System Setup**.
2. Open the **Notification Settings** tab.
3. Toggle on the notification types you want to receive.
4. Enter recipient email addresses in the **Notification Recipients** field.
5. Separate multiple addresses with commas (e.g. `manager@mybiz.com, owner@mybiz.com`).
6. Click **Save**.

All enabled notification types are sent to every email address in the recipients list.

## WhatsApp Notifications

Send automated WhatsApp messages to your customers — receipts, order confirmations, and promotional campaigns — through a registered WhatsApp Business sender.

<Note>
  WhatsApp notifications require a dedicated business phone number. You cannot use a personal number that is already active on the WhatsApp app. The registration process will disconnect that number from the standard WhatsApp application.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open WhatsApp Registration">
    Navigate to **Settings > System Setup**, then open the **WhatsApp Registration** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your business phone number">
    Type the phone number you want to register as your WhatsApp Business sender, including the country code (e.g. +254 700 000000).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify via OTP">
    Click **Send OTP**. You will receive a one-time password via SMS or voice call to the number you entered. Enter the OTP in the verification field and click **Verify**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable notification types">
    Toggle on the message types you want to send via WhatsApp:

    - **Order Confirmations** — sent to the customer when an order is placed
    - **Receipts** — sent after a payment is completed
    - **Campaign Messages** — bulk promotional messages sent via CRM Campaigns
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the registration">
    Click **Save Registration**. Your WhatsApp sender is now live and ready to send messages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once registered, any customer who provides their WhatsApp-registered phone number at checkout will automatically receive the notification types you've enabled.

## In-App Notifications

BasePoint POS also surfaces notifications directly inside the application:

- The **bell icon** in the top navigation bar shows a badge count of unread notifications.
- Click the bell to see a quick preview of recent alerts.
- Select **View All Notifications** (or navigate to the **Notifications** page) to see the complete history of all notifications, filter by type, and mark items as read.

In-app notifications include system alerts, approval requests (such as pending leave requests), and activity summaries that are relevant to the currently logged-in user's role.

<Tip>
  If you manage multiple shops, set up the daily sales summary for each location and send it to a shared management email group. You'll have a consolidated view of performance every morning without logging into each shop.
</Tip>
