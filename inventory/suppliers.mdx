The Suppliers feature in BasePoint POS gives you a central place to store all your supplier contacts and manage the full restocking cycle — from raising a purchase order to receiving goods and updating your stock levels. By keeping supplier information and purchase history in one system, you reduce the time spent chasing deliveries and make it easier to spot patterns in your purchasing.

---

## Adding a Supplier

Add each of your suppliers to BasePoint POS so you can link them to inventory items and attach them to purchase orders.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Supplier Settings">
    Navigate to **Supplier Settings** in the left sidebar (or go to `/supplier-settings`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new supplier">
    Click **Add Supplier** to open the supplier form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the supplier details">
    Complete the following fields:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Supplier Name** | The business or trading name of the supplier |
    | **Contact Person** | The name of your main point of contact at that supplier |
    | **Phone** | A direct phone number — used for quick reorder calls and WhatsApp notifications |
    | **Email** | The email address for sending purchase orders |
    | **Address** | The supplier's physical or postal address |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the supplier">
    Click **Save**. The supplier now appears in the Suppliers list and is available for selection when creating purchase orders.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Keep supplier **phone numbers** up to date. BasePoint POS can send automated WhatsApp order notifications to your suppliers when a purchase order is raised — an accurate number ensures these reach the right person without delay.
</Tip>

## Linking Suppliers to Inventory Items

Linking a default supplier to an inventory item creates a direct connection between your stock levels and the vendor who supplies that item. This makes it easier to generate accurate purchase orders when stock runs low.

To link a supplier to an inventory item:

1. Go to **Inventory** and open the item you want to update (or create a new inventory item).
2. In the item form, locate the **Supplier** field and select the supplier from the dropdown.
3. Click **Save**.

When you create a purchase order, the linked supplier is pre-populated automatically for items associated with that vendor.

<Info>
  An inventory item can have one linked supplier at a time. If you source the same item from multiple vendors, manage the alternative supplier within the purchase order at the time of ordering.
</Info>

## Creating a Purchase Order

Raise a purchase order (PO) directly in BasePoint POS to formally record what you have ordered and from whom.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Purchase Orders">
    Go to **Inventory** in the left navigation menu, then click the **Purchase Orders** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new purchase order">
    Click **New PO**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select direction and supplier">
    Choose **Supplier** as the order direction, then select the supplier from the dropdown. The supplier's contact details populate automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add line items">
    Click **Add Item** for each product you are ordering. For each line, select the **product**, enter the **quantity ordered**, and confirm the **unit cost**. Add as many lines as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the purchase order">
    Click **Submit** to finalise the order. The PO is assigned a reference number and moves to **Pending** status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Purchase order statuses

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pending" icon="clock" href="/inventory/stock-management">
    The PO has been submitted and is awaiting approval. You can still edit line items at this stage.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Approved" icon="circle-check" href="/inventory/stock-management">
    The PO has been reviewed and approved. The supplier has been notified where applicable.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Partial" icon="circle-half-stroke" href="/inventory/stock-management">
    Some items have been received but the order is not yet complete. Outstanding quantities remain open.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Delivered" icon="truck" href="/inventory/stock-management">
    All ordered items have been received and the purchase order is fully closed.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  A **Cancelled** status is also available. Once a PO is cancelled it cannot be re-opened — create a new PO if you need to reorder.
</Note>

## Receiving Stock

When a delivery arrives, update the purchase order status to reflect what was received.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the open purchase order">
    Go to **Inventory > Purchase Orders** and locate the PO using the supplier name or PO reference number.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the delivery status">
    Open the PO and use the status action menu to set it to **Approved** if confirming the full order, or **Partially Delivered** if only part of the delivery came through.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Confirm**. Stock levels for all received products update immediately. A partial delivery leaves the PO open so you can record the remaining items when they arrive.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Receiving a purchase order creates an entry in the inventory audit trail, keeping your stock history clean and traceable back to the original order.
</Note>

## Supplier Reports

Review your purchase history and monitor outstanding orders for each supplier without leaving BasePoint POS.

<Accordion title="View outstanding purchase orders">
  1. Go to **Inventory > Purchase Orders**.
  2. Filter by **Status: Pending** or **Status: Partial** to see all open orders.
  3. Use the **Supplier** filter to narrow the list to a specific vendor.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Export purchase data">
  From the Purchase Orders list, click **Export** and choose **Export Excel** or **Export PDF** to download your PO records. Use this for reconciling invoices or sharing purchasing data with your accountant.
</Accordion>
