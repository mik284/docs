Keeping accurate stock records prevents over-selling, reduces waste, and gives you confidence in your purchasing decisions. BasePoint POS tracks inventory in real time — every sale automatically deducts from the relevant product's stock count, and every purchase order receipt or manual update creates a timestamped entry in the ledger. Whether you run a single café or a multi-branch retail chain, the Inventory module gives you the visibility you need to stay stocked and profitable.

---

## Viewing Stock Levels

The **Inventory** screen gives you a live snapshot of everything in your stockroom.

1. Click **Inventory** in the left navigation menu (or go to `/inventory`).
2. The stock list shows each tracked item with its **current quantity**, **minimum stock level**, **supplier price**, and **usage type**.
3. Use the search bar or category filter at the top to narrow the list to a specific product group.
4. Watch for the **Low Stock** indicator — items at or below their minimum stock level are flagged automatically so you can plan your next purchase order.

<Info>
  Only inventory items with **Activate Inventory** enabled appear in the Inventory list. If a product is missing, open it in **Store** and toggle on **Activate Inventory**.
</Info>

## Updating Stock Quantities

To correct a recorded quantity — for example after a physical count, a new delivery, or a write-off — edit the inventory item directly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the item">
    Go to **Inventory** and locate the item you want to update. Use the search bar or category filter to find it quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the edit form">
    Click the **edit** button on the item row to open the inventory item form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the quantity">
    In the **Inventory & Pricing** section, update the **Current Quantity** field to the correct figure. You can also update the **Minimum Stock Level** at this step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the changes">
    Click **Save**. The stock level updates immediately and the change is recorded with a timestamp.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Quantity changes made by editing an inventory item take effect immediately. If you need to record the reason for a stock change (for example, wastage or damage), note it in the item's **Description** field before saving.
</Warning>

## Material Transfers

If your business operates multiple shop locations, you can move stock from one branch to another directly within BasePoint POS.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Material Transfers">
    Go to **Inventory** and click the **Material Transfers** tab at the top of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new transfer">
    Click **New Transfer** (the **+** button in the toolbar).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the source and destination shops">
    Select the **From Shop (Source)** (the branch sending the stock) and the **To Shop (Destination)** (the branch receiving it). The two shops must be different.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add items and quantities">
    Click **Add Item**, select the source product and its matching destination product, then enter the quantity to transfer. Add more lines for additional products in the same transfer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the transfer">
    Click **Submit**. Stock is deducted from the source shop immediately. The destination shop's count updates once the transfer is confirmed as received.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The receiving shop must **confirm receipt** of the transfer before their stock count updates. This two-step process prevents discrepancies if goods are still in transit.
</Note>

## Low Stock Alerts

Set a minimum stock level on each inventory item so BasePoint POS can warn you before you run out.

- **Set a minimum stock level:** Open an inventory item in **Inventory**, click **Edit**, and enter a value in the **Minimum Stock Level** field. For example, entering `10` means the item is flagged as low stock when the quantity drops to 10 or below.
- **View low-stock items:** In **Inventory**, look for items highlighted with the low-stock warning indicator — these are all items at or below their minimum stock level.
- **Notification settings:** Navigate to **System Setup > Notification Settings** to configure automated low-stock alerts, including WhatsApp and email notification channels.

<Tip>
  Review low-stock items every morning before your business opens. Catching shortages early gives you time to contact suppliers and arrange deliveries before stock runs out during service.
</Tip>

## Purchase Orders

Create a formal purchase order (PO) to record stock you have ordered from a supplier. When goods arrive, updating the PO status automatically records the delivery against your inventory.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Purchase Orders">
    Go to **Inventory** and click the **Purchase Orders** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new PO">
    Click **New PO**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the supplier and direction">
    Choose whether this is a **Supplier** order (incoming stock) or a **Customer** order (outgoing), then select the supplier from the dropdown. If the supplier is not listed, add them first under **Supplier Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add line items">
    Click **Add Item** and select the product, quantity ordered, and agreed unit cost. Repeat for each product in the order.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the purchase order">
    Click **Submit**. The PO is created with a **Pending** status and appears in the Purchase Orders list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve and receive the goods">
    When the order is confirmed, open the PO and update its status to **Approved**. When the delivery arrives, mark it as **Partially Delivered** if only some items arrived, or as **Delivered** when the full order is received.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use the **Export** button on the Purchase Orders list to download all PO records as an Excel or PDF file. Use this for reconciling supplier invoices or sharing purchasing data with your accountant.
</Tip>
