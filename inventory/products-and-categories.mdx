Your product catalog in BasePoint POS is the foundation of everything sold at your business. Categories act as the organising structure — grouping related items together and powering the sidebar navigation in your POS screen — while products are the individual items your staff ring up during a sale. Setting up a clean, well-structured catalog makes serving customers faster and reporting more meaningful.

---

## Creating Categories

Categories appear as tiles in the POS menu sidebar, so creating them first makes it easier to assign products as you add them. BasePoint POS supports a two-level hierarchy: **Main Categories** and **Sub-categories**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Category Settings">
    Navigate to **Category Settings** in the left sidebar (or go to `/category-settings`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new category">
    Click **Add Category** to open the category creation form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the category details">
    Enter a **Category Name**, choose an **icon** from the icon picker, and assign a **colour** to help staff identify the category at a glance on the POS screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add sub-categories (optional)">
    Switch to the **Sub-Category** tab and click **Add Sub-category**. Give the sub-category a name, link it to a main category, and save. Repeat for as many sub-categories as you need.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the category">
    Click **Save**. The new category becomes available immediately when assigning products and appears in the POS menu sidebar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Only **Main Categories** appear as top-level tiles in the POS menu sidebar. Sub-categories are accessible by tapping the main category tile, making it easy to keep the POS screen uncluttered even with a large menu.
</Note>

## Adding Products

With your categories in place, you can start building your product list. Each product you create is immediately available for sale in the POS and can optionally be tracked in inventory.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Store">
    Click **Store** in the left navigation menu to open your product catalog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Add Product form">
    Click **Add New Product** in the top-right corner of the Store page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the product details">
    Complete the product fields (see the full field reference below). At minimum, enter a **Name**, **Price**, **Category**, and **VAT Type**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a product image (optional)">
    Drag a photo onto the thumbnail area or click to browse and select a file. Images must be under 5 MB. A clear product image helps staff quickly confirm items during a sale.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable inventory tracking (optional)">
    Toggle **Activate Inventory** on if you want BasePoint POS to deduct stock each time this product is sold.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the product">
    Click **Add New Product** (or **Edit Product** when updating). The product appears in the Store and is immediately available in the POS under the assigned category.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Product field reference

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Name" icon="tag" href="/inventory/products-and-categories">
    The display name shown on the POS screen and receipts. Keep it short and descriptive.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Price" icon="circle-dollar-sign" href="/inventory/products-and-categories">
    The price charged to the customer. Supports decimal values to two places.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Category" icon="folder" href="/inventory/products-and-categories">
    The category this product belongs to. Determines where it appears in the POS sidebar.
  </Card>
  <Card title="VAT Type" icon="percent" href="/inventory/products-and-categories">
    Tax classification for the product — **Standard** (16%), **Zero Rated** (0%), or **Exempt** (0%).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Activate Inventory" icon="warehouse" href="/inventory/stock-management">
    Toggle on to have BasePoint POS automatically deduct stock each time this product is sold.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Addons (optional)" icon="puzzle-piece" href="/inventory/products-and-categories">
    Modifier groups that let staff capture customer customisations at the point of sale — for example, *Extra Cheese* or *No Onions*. Configure modifier groups first under **Category Settings > Modifiers**.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Description" icon="align-left" href="/inventory/products-and-categories">
    An optional internal note or description visible to staff on the product detail screen.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Image" icon="image" href="/inventory/products-and-categories">
    A photo or illustration of the product displayed on the POS tile. Maximum 5 MB.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Bulk Import

If you have an existing product list in a spreadsheet, you can import all your products at once instead of adding them one by one.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the import template">
    Go to **Store**, click the **Import** button, and download the Excel template (`.xlsx`). The template includes all supported columns with example values.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the template">
    Open the file in a spreadsheet application and populate each row with your product data. The required columns are **Product Name**, **Price**, and **Category**. Quantity and Description are optional but recommended.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the completed file">
    Return to **Store > Import**, drag your completed `.xlsx` file onto the upload area (or click to browse), and wait for BasePoint POS to analyse the file. A column-mapping preview confirms which fields were recognised.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the import">
    Review the preview for any errors flagged in red, correct them in the spreadsheet if needed, and click **Confirm** to finalise. BasePoint POS reports how many products were created, updated, or skipped.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use the **Export** button on the Store page to download your existing products as an Excel file. This is a quick way to bulk-edit prices or descriptions — update the exported file, then re-import it.
</Tip>

---

<Tip>
  Use a consistent naming convention across your entire catalog — for example, *"Category – Variant – Size"* (e.g., *"Beverage – Coke – 500ml"*). Consistent names make the search bar in the POS much faster to use during a busy service.
</Tip>

<Note>
  **Modifiers and add-ons** can be attached to any product to let staff capture customer customisations at the point of sale — for example, *Extra Cheese*, *No Onions*, or *Large Size*. Configure modifier groups under **Category Settings > Modifiers**, then link them to individual products when adding or editing a product.
</Note>
