Bank reconciliation is the process of comparing your accounting records in Pesa against the actual transactions on your bank statement. Reconciling regularly ensures that every deposit, withdrawal, and bank charge is accounted for — and helps you catch discrepancies such as duplicate entries, missing payments, or unauthorized transactions before they cause larger problems.

## Importing a Bank Statement

Before you can reconcile, upload your bank statement into Pesa. Bank statement imports and reconciliation are handled on separate pages — import first, then reconcile.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Bank Statements">
    Navigate to **Accounting > Bank Statements**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the import">
    Click **Import Statement**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the bank account">
    Choose the bank account in Pesa that the statement belongs to. If the account does not exist yet, create it first under **Accounting > Accounts** as an Asset account with the bank account sub-type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your file">
    Click **Upload File** and select your bank statement exported from your online banking portal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and categorize">
    Pesa loads the imported transactions and shows them for review. Each transaction is categorized automatically where possible; uncategorized items are flagged for your attention. Assign an account to any uncategorized transaction before pushing them to your books.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Push to books">
    Once you are satisfied with the categorization, push the transactions to your journal entries. The import status updates to **Fully Pushed** when all lines have been processed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Bank statement imports move through the following statuses: **Processing** (the file is being parsed), **Review** (transactions need your attention before being pushed), **Partially Pushed** (some transactions have been pushed to the books), **Fully Pushed** (all transactions are in your books), and **Voided** (the import has been cancelled). Voiding an import does not reverse transactions that have already been pushed.
</Note>

## Running Reconciliation

Once your statement transactions are in your books, reconcile the bank account to confirm everything matches.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Reconciliation">
    Navigate to **Accounting > Reconciliation**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new session">
    Click **New Session** and select the **bank account** and **statement period** (start and end date) you want to reconcile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review unmatched transactions">
    Pesa displays all bank statement lines alongside your recorded journal entries. Lines that have not yet been matched are highlighted. The running difference between your statement balance and your book balance is shown as you work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Match transactions">
    For each bank statement line, either:
    - **Select a matching recorded entry** — click the line and choose the corresponding journal entry or receipt from the suggested matches.
    - **Create a new entry** — if no matching entry exists in Pesa, post it on the spot (useful for bank charges or interest credits).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the reconciliation">
    Once all lines are matched and the difference reads **0.00**, click **Complete Reconciliation**. Pesa locks the session and records its completion date.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handling Unmatched Transactions

Not every bank line will have an obvious match. When you encounter an unmatched transaction, you have three options:

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Transaction exists in Pesa under a slightly different date or amount | Find and select it manually from the match list |
| Unrecorded bank charge or fee | Create a new expense journal entry for the charge |
| Unrecorded income (e.g., bank interest) | Create a new income journal entry |
| Duplicate entry in your books | Void the duplicate entry in Pesa, then match the remaining entry to the bank line |

<Tip>
  Reconcile at the end of every month, ideally within a few days of receiving your statement. Monthly reconciliation keeps the number of unmatched items manageable and ensures your financial reports are accurate before you share them with accountants, investors, or tax authorities.
</Tip>

## Reconciliation History

View all past reconciliation sessions by navigating to **Accounting > Reconciliation**. Each session shows:

- The bank account name
- The statement period covered
- The opening and closing balances
- The number of matched and unmatched transactions
- Status: **Open**, **In Progress**, **Completed**, or **Voided**

Click any session to open its workspace and review the matched transactions. If you need to undo a completed reconciliation (for example, to correct a mismatched entry), select it and choose **Void** — this cancels the session so you can re-open the period for corrections.
