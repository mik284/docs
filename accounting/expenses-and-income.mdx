Pesa gives you a complete picture of money flowing in and out of your business. Sales revenue flows in automatically from the POS, but many expenses, supplier invoices, and non-sale income streams require manual recording. Use the Expenses, Bills, Income, and Petty Cash pages to capture everything the POS does not record automatically — so your Profit & Loss report reflects the true cost and earnings of running your business.

## Recording an Expense

Use Expenses to record direct business costs such as rent, utilities, salaries, and marketing spend.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Expenses">
    Navigate to **Accounting > Expenses**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new expense">
    Click **Add Expense** to open the expense form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the expense details">
    Complete the following fields:
    - **Date** — the date the expense was incurred
    - **Expense Account** — the COA account to charge (e.g., *Rent Expense*, *Utilities*)
    - **Amount** — the total cost
    - **Description** — a brief note explaining the expense
    - **Reference** — a receipt number, invoice reference, or internal code
    - **Vendor** — the supplier or payee (optional)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The expense is created with a **Draft** status. An approver can then review and approve it, which posts the corresponding journal entry.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Common expense categories to set up in your chart of accounts include:

- Rent & Premises
- Utilities (Electricity, Water, Internet)
- Salaries & Wages
- Marketing & Advertising
- Packaging & Supplies
- Repairs & Maintenance
- Transport & Delivery

<Tip>
  Categorize every expense against the correct account from the start. Consistent categorization is what makes your Profit & Loss report meaningful — if supplies, repairs, and salaries all land in a generic "Expenses" bucket, you lose the visibility needed to control costs.
</Tip>

## Managing Bills (Supplier Invoices)

Bills are invoices you receive from suppliers for goods or services delivered on credit. Recording bills in Pesa lets you track what you owe and when payments are due.

Navigate to **Accounting > Bills** to view all supplier invoices. Bills move through the following statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Saved but not yet confirmed — editable |
| **Pending** | Confirmed and awaiting payment |
| **Paid** | Payment has been recorded against the bill |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Bills">
    Navigate to **Accounting > Bills**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new bill">
    Click **Add Bill**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter bill details">
    Fill in:
    - **Supplier** — select or create the supplier
    - **Invoice Date** — the date on the supplier's invoice
    - **Due Date** — the payment deadline
    - **Line Items** — for each item, select the expense account and enter the amount
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Once confirmed, the bill status moves to **Pending** and the amount appears as a liability in your accounts payable balance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recording Income

Use the Income page for revenue that does not come through the POS — for example, consulting fees, rental income from a sub-let space, or interest received.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Income">
    Navigate to **Accounting > Income**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add income">
    Click **Add Income**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter income details">
    Fill in:
    - **Date** — the date the income was received or earned
    - **Income Account** — the revenue account to credit (e.g., *Rental Income*, *Consulting Fees*)
    - **Amount** — the amount received
    - **Description** — a brief note describing the source
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The income entry is posted and immediately reflected in your revenue totals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Petty Cash

Petty cash covers small, day-to-day cash expenditures that are too minor to process through a formal expense or bill — for example, buying printer paper or paying for a taxi fare.

Navigate to **Accounting > Petty Cash** to manage your petty cash fund. For each transaction, record:

- **Amount** — the cash spent
- **Purpose** — what the money was used for
- **Authorized by** — the team member who approved the spend

The petty cash page shows a **running balance** so you always know how much cash remains in the fund. When the fund runs low, record a **top-up** to replenish it from your main cash or bank account — this creates the corresponding journal entry automatically.

<Info>
  Set a maximum balance for your petty cash fund and top it up in fixed amounts (e.g., always replenish to KES 5,000). This makes petty cash easier to audit and reduces the risk of untracked cash accumulating.
</Info>

## Sales Receipts

Navigate to **Accounting > Sales Receipts** to view a full record of all POS transactions as income entries. Every time a cashier completes an order, a sales receipt is automatically created in Pesa with the order total, payment method, and the income account it was posted to.

You do not need to create sales receipts manually. Use this page to:

- Search and review individual POS transactions
- Verify that a specific sale was correctly recorded as income
- Cross-reference receipts during bank reconciliation to match card or mobile money deposits
