BasePoint POS is built for businesses that operate across more than one physical location. Under a single admin account you can create and manage multiple shop locations — each one behaves as an independent operation with its own product catalogue, tables, staff assignments, payment methods, and reporting data. At the same time, everything rolls up into one admin dashboard so you always have a cross-location view without logging in and out of separate accounts.

## How Multi-Shop Works

Every shop in your account is a fully self-contained unit. When a staff member logs in and selects a shop, they work entirely within that shop's context — their cart, orders, tables, and reports are all scoped to that location. Nothing bleeds across to other shops unless you explicitly configure shared resources.

Each shop maintains its own:

- **Product catalogue and menu** — prices, categories, modifiers, and addons can differ per location
- **Table layout** — floor plans and table slots are configured independently per shop
- **Staff assignments** — a staff member can be assigned to one shop or multiple shops
- **Payment methods** — each shop can accept different payment types (cash, card, M-Pesa, etc.)
- **Printer settings** — receipt printers and kitchen printers are configured per shop
- **Reports and revenue data** — sales figures, shift reports, and inventory usage are tracked separately per location

All shops share the **same admin dashboard**, so an Admin or Manager can see every location from one login.

<Note>
  Reports in the **Admin Dashboard** aggregate or allow filtering across all shops, giving you cross-location revenue, order volume, and inventory visibility. Reports accessed from within an individual shop's workspace are scoped to that shop only. Make sure your staff understand which view they're looking at before drawing business conclusions from report data.
</Note>

## Switching Between Shops

The shop switcher lives in the **top navigation bar** of the admin dashboard. Clicking it shows a list of all shops on your account. Selecting a shop reloads the workspace into that shop's context — the data and available settings all update to reflect the selected location.

From the admin dashboard you can also click **Open Shop** on any shop card to jump directly into its POS or module workspace. Where **Open Shop** takes you depends on which modules are active for your account — you'll land on the most relevant workspace for your configuration.

<Tip>
  If you manage several locations and need to identify them at a glance, ask your administrator to use distinct shop names that include the location — for example, "Garden City Branch" or "Westlands Outlet". The shop name appears in the switcher, on printed receipts, and in report headers, making it easy for staff to confirm they're working in the right location.
</Tip>

## Adding a New Shop

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Admin Dashboard → Shop Management">
    From the admin dashboard, navigate to **Shop Management**. You'll see a list (or map view) of all existing shops on your account, along with summary cards showing each shop's POS mode, location, staff count, and today's revenue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Shop">
    Click the **Add Shop** button in the toolbar. The shop creation modal opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the shop details">
    Enter the following information for the new location:
    - **Shop name** — a clear, identifiable name (e.g., "Karen Branch")
    - **Address / Location** — the physical street address; BasePoint POS supports Google Maps address lookup for precise geo-coordinates
    - **Contact details** — phone number and any relevant contact info for the location
    - **POS mode** — choose the operating mode that matches the location type: **Duka Services** (restaurant/food service), **Retail**, **Hospital**, or **Hotel**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign staff members to the new shop">
    After the shop is created, go to **Settings → Staff & Users** and assign the relevant staff members to this shop. A staff member must be assigned to a shop before they can log into its POS workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure products, tables, and payment methods">
    Complete the shop setup by configuring:
    - **Products and categories** — add your menu or product catalogue for this location
    - **Tables** — set up the floor plan and table slots if this is a restaurant or hotel
    - **Payment methods** — enable the payment types accepted at this location (cash, card, M-Pesa, etc.)
    - **Printer settings** — configure receipt and kitchen printers if applicable
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Admin Dashboard vs Shop Workspace

BasePoint POS separates the **admin dashboard** from individual **shop workspaces**, and the distinction matters for both data visibility and permissions.

### Admin Dashboard

The admin dashboard gives you a **cross-shop view** of your entire account. From here you can:

- View all shops and their summary metrics in one place
- See a map of all shop locations (shops with GPS coordinates are plotted on Google Maps)
- Manage users and roles that apply across locations
- Access system-level settings and module configuration
- Compare revenue and order data across locations

Only users with the **Admin** role (or a custom role with the relevant permissions) can access admin dashboard features.

### Shop Workspace

The shop workspace is **scoped to the currently selected shop**. When a staff member opens a shop, all screens — tables, products, orders, accounting, and reports — reflect only that location's data.

A Cashier or Waiter who is assigned to Shop A cannot navigate to Shop B's data — their session is locked to their assigned location.

<Info>
  Inventory **Transfers** are the mechanism for moving stock between shops. If you manage multiple locations with shared inventory, use **Inventory → Transfers** to create a transfer request between shops. The receiving shop's Inventory Clerk can then approve and complete the transfer from their shop's workspace.
</Info>

## Shop Modes Reference

When you create a shop you select a POS mode. The mode affects how the POS workspace is labeled and which operational flows are emphasized, but all shops on your account share the same underlying platform.

| POS Mode | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Duka Services** | Restaurants, cafés, food courts | Full table management, kitchen routing, and recipe-based inventory |
| **Retail** | Shops, boutiques, convenience stores | Optimized for quick product-based transactions without table management |
| **Hospital** | Clinics, pharmacies, healthcare outlets | Consultation and patient-linked order flows |
| **Hotel** | Hotel F&B, room service, front desk | Room-based table mapping and hotel-specific order routing |

<Tip>
  You can run different shop modes within the same account. For example, a hospitality group might have a **Hotel** shop for room service, a **Duka Services** shop for their restaurant, and a **Retail** shop for their gift store — all managed from one BasePoint POS admin account.
</Tip>
